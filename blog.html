With the first quote, I didn't realize that lung cancer was the most common. The following two quotes I found interesting because I never really thought about cooking smoke being an issue.

Lung cancer is diagnosed about 220,000 times each year in the United States and causes more deaths annually than the next three cancers combinedâ€”those of the colon, breast, and pancreas.

China found that smokers who stir-fried meat every day had nearly three times the odds of lung cancer compared to smokers who stir-fried foods other than meat on a daily basis. This is thought to be because of a group of carcinogens called heterocyclic amines that are formed when muscle tissue is subjected to high temperatures.

If you do need to be around frying bacon or eggs, it would be safer to limit your exposure by using a backyard grill. Studies show that the number of particles deposited into the lungs increases by a factor of ten when frying indoors versus outdoors.
